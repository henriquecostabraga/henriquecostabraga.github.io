# Materials Engineering Research

## Overview

My research in Materials Engineering spans more than three decades, combining physical metallurgy, industrial process engineering, experimental investigation, and applied engineering research.

The trajectory began with studies on the hot mechanical behavior of metallic materials, thermomechanical processing, and dynamic recrystallization. It subsequently expanded toward industrial metallurgical processes, silicon metal production and refining, hydrometallurgy, cementitious materials, and nanomaterials.

A recurring characteristic of this work is the connection between fundamental engineering phenomena and real-world materials and process problems.

---

## Cementitious Materials and Nanomaterials

Current research activities also include cementitious materials and nanomaterials.

Recent investigations address the incorporation of carbon nanotubes into cement-based materials and the resulting effects on material behavior and performance.

This research expands my Materials Engineering activities toward advanced construction materials and nanoscale modification of engineering materials.

---

## Industrial Metallurgy and Silicon Metal Processing

My industrial metallurgical research included direct involvement with silicon metal production and refining processes.

The work addressed raw material control, refining operations, synthetic slag models, and optimization of sampling procedures for refined liquid silicon metal. Several of these investigations were developed in connection with industrial production at the Eletrosilex plant and presented at national and international metallurgical conferences.

These studies reflect a strongly applied phase of my engineering career, in which process control, metallurgical modeling, sampling methodology, and industrial production problems were investigated in an operational environment.

---

## Dynamic Recrystallization and Steel Processing

A major early research line investigated dynamic recrystallization during the hot working of steels.

The studies explored stress-strain curve modeling as a means of identifying and analyzing the occurrence of dynamic recrystallization during hot rolling.

This work also established a long-standing interest in microstructural phenomena and their quantitative description. This interest would later intersect with computational microstructure modeling and the development of the **SimCres** framework.

## Hydrometallurgy and Industrial Process Engineering

Research in hydrometallurgy addressed the treatment and valorization of steel pickling liquors.

A microwave-hydrothermal process was investigated for the simultaneous production of impurity-free water and magnetite from industrial steel pickling solutions.

This work extended my research toward industrial residue processing, aqueous metallurgical systems, material recovery, and process sustainability.

---

## Hot Deformation and Thermomechanical Processing

Early research focused on the mechanical behavior of metallic materials under hot deformation conditions.

Studies addressed aluminum alloys, titanium, and Ti-6Al-4V, with emphasis on stress-strain behavior, axial compression, and the response of metallic materials at elevated temperatures.

This research established an early connection between physical metallurgy, mechanical behavior, and the quantitative interpretation of deformation phenomena.
